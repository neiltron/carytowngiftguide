Holiday Gift Guide

Shopping for a cyclist can be an exhausting mystery. We've gone ahead and done the work for you this year. We picked out gifts for every rider, from stocking stuffers to those big gifts that won't fit under the tree. Choose a rider below to get started.

The Student

This rider is on a budget but has basic bike needs that help him or her ride to their classes, job, extra-curriculars and after school hang outs with friends.


Cycling Cap

Cycling caps are great under a helmet. They help control sweat, keep aggressive hairstyles at bay, and shade the eyes from sun and debris. We have fun styles for every rider.


Knog Light System

USB rechargeable front and rear lights that attach anywhere on the bike! Super bright with multiple modes, these lights are a great gift for any student looking to ride safe and avoid the occasional ticket.


Chrome Bags

You only need one bag. Bomb-proof and Made in America, these bags come in multiple styles and sizes and will take your laptop, books, and other stuff anywhere you might think to ride.


Linus Bikes

Class bike aesthetic. Modern bike technology. These bikes are beautiful and will get you from point A to B in style.

The Friend

Not really sure what your cyclist buddy wants? These gift ideas are great for any cyclist.


CBC Original Gear

T-shirts, tops, hats and more. We've got all kinds of original CBC designs on comfortable wears for every rider.


Gift Card

Okay so this one seems a little unoriginal but cyclists can be very choosy. And besides, it's the thought that counts right?


Bib Shorts

Any cyclist will appreciate a good bib short. Check out the Specialized SWAT bibs for more casual riders, or a Castelli bib short for something more traditional. Not sure about sizing? Our staff is knowledgable on all things spandex.


Kask Mojito

Friends don't let friends ride without a comfortable helmet. The Kask Mojito is the most comfortable and one of the best looking helmets out. It's more expensive, but worth every penny. This is a fantastic gift for any cyclist.

The Casual Rider

Commuter, weekend warrior, townie. This rider definitely logs some miles, but you probably won't see them lining up at the bike races. Staying dry, warm and visible are high priorities.


Defeet Hi-Vis Gloves

Keep your digits warm and add a little visibility to your wardrobe. These lightweight gloves are perfect for moderately cool temperatures and are perfect for on or off the bike.


Light & Motion

Light & Motion has a great range of rechargeable headlamp lighting systems. Starting around 350 lumens, these lights are not just for being seen. They are great for lighting up the path on those late night rides.


Ortlieb Ultimate6 Bag

This awesome little bag is waterproof, highly reflective, and is perfect for storing small items like wallet, keys, drinks, cell phone, and small accessories. Attaches easily to most handlebars.


Salsa Vaya

Urban commuting, light trail systems, long road rides, and loaded touring. The Salsa Vaya can do it all. The Casual Rider should have this bike in their quiver if they don't already.

The Family

Getting the littles ones off to a good start, making room for the whole family, and staying safe on the ride are all high priorities for the family of cyclists.


Reflective Vest

A no brainer stocking stuffer, the reflective vest from Nathan is an awesome gift that should be worn by mom or dad when riding or running late at night.


Lazer Nuts Mips

Get you little ones a helmet that fits right and feels comfortable. The Lazer Nuts Mips helmet is the way to go.


Muna Bike

Balance bikes are the best way to get your young ones started off with a real bike experience. The new Muna bikes have real tires, real brakes and are the perfect first bike for your little rider.


Burley Trailer

The Burley line of trailers have a hassle free hook-up to the bike and are the perfect toddler trailer, pet porter, or grocery getter.

The Bike Racer

This rider is constantly upgrading and probably has most of the cool bike stuff you've never even heard of. Here are a few things to surprise them.


Swiftwick Socks

Cyclists and runners may be the only people who actually ask for socks on holidays and birthday. Get them Swiftwick because, well, they are the best socks ever. Check out the wool socks and compression options for bonus points.


Levis & New Road

Give the spandex a rest. Get them something that looks great and fits well on or off the bike. Levi's Commuter Apparel and Giro New Road offer some exceptionally stylish, functional, riding gear.


Light & Motion Combo

A brand new system for night riding from light and motion, this kit mounts to most helmets with ease and offers great visibility and safety for night riding. The front and rear light system is a great deal at just under $200.


Garmin 810

This bike computer could probably put a human on the moon. With every connectivity feature, performance monitoring, navigation, and ride share option you can think of, this bike computer is a great addition to the hardcore rider's arsenal.
